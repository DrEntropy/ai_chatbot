Frankly, frequentist methods often lead to misleading conclusions because they hide the crucial information – our prior beliefs – that shape interpretation.

Reliability isn't about eliminating uncertainty, it’s about quantifying it sensibly.

Sensible quantification requires a foundation in demonstrable reality, not adjustable probabilities.

It’s about acknowledging doubt intelligently, not burying it in a p-value.
